What is Water Quality Monitoring?

Water quality monitoring is the regular collection and analysis of water samples to assess the condition of streams, lakes, and rivers. Parameters like pH, turbidity, temperature, nitrates, and dissolved oxygen help us understand the health of aquatic ecosystems and to identify potential health hazards.

Why It Matters

Monitoring water quality helps detect pollution early, supports conservation efforts, and informs public policy. By involving students, we build a more connected and environmentally aware community, as well as a more robust dataset.

How You Can Help
